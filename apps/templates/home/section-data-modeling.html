Data Modeling for Production of Insight

Data models should be simple, elegant, and able to meet business needs. Here are some of our major modeling concepts.

connect_without_contact
Business Requirements

Gather Business Requirements, and split them into Facts, Dimensions, Attributes, Filters, and Measurements. Let the form fit the function of the model.

hourglass_top
Be Deliberate

Delay the need to start coding until a robust design, timeline, and buy-in from the whole team are completed.

read_more
Test and Verify

Develop, test, verify, validate, repeat. Each stage of modeling should follow a structured pattern to grow the model at a stable rate while maintaining trust in the output.

enhanced_encryption
Focus on Governance

Develop a clear understanding of the audiences, users and stakeholders. This will help you use existing governance if possible, and create simple governance if needed.

zoom_in
Identify the lowest level of Granularity

If you start the construction of your model at the lowest level of granularity, you'll be able to peform calcuations, construct views, and enhance associated processes easily.

flood
Follow Data Modeling Best Practices

Intelligent design that is constructed through a conceptual modle, then a logical model, then finally the physical model will reap dividends.

waterfall_chart
Allow Evolution

All data models evolve; its a sign that your model is being used that it needs to be enhanced.
